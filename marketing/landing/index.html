How it works

Three things ChatGPT can't do

A chatbot in a browser tab can't touch your operating system. Mins Bot can.

Operates your apps

It sees your screen, clicks buttons, fills forms, drags files, sends keystrokes. Anything you can do with mouse and keyboard, it can do — autonomously or on command.

Remembers across sessions

Episodic memory, life profile, custom skills — all saved as plain markdown on your machine. The longer it runs, the more it knows you. Switching cost compounds.

Runs while you sleep

File-driven recurring tasks and scheduled reports. The 4 AM morning brief generates whether you're at your desk or not — and waits to deliver until you're actually back.

JARVIS-style barge-in

Speaks replies aloud. Stops mid-sentence the moment you start to talk. Turns continuous screen-watching on/off with one click — observes only when you ask.

Extensible by markdown

Drop a .md file in ~/mins_bot_data/skills/ with steps to follow — that's a new skill. No coding required. Same for recurring tasks and scheduled reports.

Local-first by default

Your screen, your files, your audio — never leave your machine unless you explicitly enable a cloud integration. No telemetry, no tracking, no engagement metrics phoned home.

FAQ

Common questions

What does it actually do that I can't do already?

Three things, concretely: (1) it operates your apps autonomously — open Chrome, find a tab, fill a form, drag a file, send a message, all from a single instruction; (2) it remembers your work across sessions in plain markdown on your machine; (3) it runs scheduled background jobs and delivers the results when you're actually at your desk.

Is my screen / data being sent to a server?

By default, no. The vision/LLM calls go to whatever provider's API key you supply (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, or local Ollama). All memory and skill files live on your machine in ~/mins_bot_data/. Nothing is sent to Mins Bot the company, ever.

Do I need to bring my own API key?

For now, yes — alpha users supply OpenAI / Anthropic / Gemini keys directly. Paid Pro tier (later) will include managed API access at a flat monthly rate.

Which OS?

Windows for v1 (the local OS-control layer is Windows-specific today). macOS and Linux are on the roadmap once paying users justify the build.

How is this different from Apple Intelligence / Microsoft Copilot?

Both of those are sandboxed to a small set of first-party experiences and run on the OS vendor's terms. Mins Bot runs on your terms — open architecture, plain-markdown extensibility, and your data stays put. Closer to "what JARVIS would have looked like if it shipped instead of staying in a movie."
